Metrics and Principles of Codec Operation

Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR): Mathematical metric measuring the level of distortion (errors) during compression. Values above 30 dB are acceptable, and above 40 dB mean distortions are almost imperceptible to the human eye.

Structural Similarity Index (SSIM): Metric simulating human perception. Unlike PSNR, it evaluates the preservation of structure, brightness, and contrast of textures. Measured from 0 to 1 (where 1 is an exact match).

Time Asymmetry (Encoder/Decoder): The hybrid algorithm is asymmetric. Compression takes milliseconds due to lightweight DWT math, ideal for weak edge devices. Restoration takes longer, using CNN tensor operations on a powerful receiver.

Wavelet Decomposition (DWT): Discrete wavelet transform decomposes the image into frequencies. The algorithm discards high-frequency noise and transmits only the low-frequency "skeleton" (LL), reducing data size by 4, 16, or 64 times.

Neural Synthesis: Convolutional neural networks (ESPCN / FSRCNN) on the receiver side reconstruct the discarded high-frequency details, inferring object structures.

Hybrid Metadata: To eliminate neural network flaws (color shift and micro-contrast smoothing), the encoder sends lightweight vectors (Color Palette and Canny Edge Map). Metadata weighs kilobytes but radically increases SSIM.
